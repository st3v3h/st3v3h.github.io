01 About

A scientist who got pulled toward the data.

In 2008 I finished a Co-op Biochemistry degree at the University of Guelph, and was named the university's Co-op Student of the Year in Science & Engineering after a productive term in the Jones Lab. I stayed on with a CIHR Master's Award and graduated with an M.Sc. in Molecular Biology / Neuroscience at the end of 2010.

After a few years at high-performance research labs in Canada and the US, I joined CAMH in 2016 and pivoted from bench work to clinical data management — building large, multi-user databases for the Slaight Family Centre for Youth in Transition. Two years later I became REDCap Project Lead, overseeing REDCap across research, clinical trials, and clinical data capture.

In 2022 I joined Alphabet's health-tech organization, Verily, as a Product Data Manager. I also teach a REDCap class at Seneca College. To sharpen the strategic side of the job, I picked up a second Master's — the Executive MHI at the University of Toronto — finishing in 2020.
